Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

A helmet needs replacement if it is generally older than five years, even if it looks fine, because the protective materials naturally break down over time.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

It is generally safe to ride, but you should always perform a thorough safety check first, especially if it was stored for a long time or in a non-ideal location (like a damp shed).

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

The most important rule is to obey all traffic laws just like a car, including stopping at red lights and stop signs, and always riding with the flow of traffic. ou must wear a properly fitted helmet on every ride for head protection and strive to be visible by wearing bright clothing and using lights, especially at night. To ensure you are predictable, always use hand signals when you plan to turn or slow down, and stay alert for opening car doors and road hazards. Finally, make sure to ride far enough from the curb so you are seen, but pull over to let traffic pass when safe.
